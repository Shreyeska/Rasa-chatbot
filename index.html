    Nepali Women in Politics: Success and Challenges

    January 2024 by Ghimire Law Associates

    A new report on a training programme for women in hashtag Nepal’s legal profession has been released by the International Bar Association (IBA). The report was commissioned by the IBA and written by New Perimeter , the nonprofit affiliate of law firm DLA Piper, an IBA Group member. It includes the project’s goals, successes, and challenges. The report, International training of women lawyers in Nepal: A case study, reveals only 12 per cent of Nepali hashtag#lawyers are women (approximately 2,200) and there exists a gender bias against women that is similar to that seen in other jurisdictions.

    Nepal Government Increases Minimum Wages for Workers

    Aug. 27, 2023, by Arch Legal Services

    Ensuring fair compensation for employees is an important aspect of maintaining a fair and equitable work environment. Nepal Government recently took a significant step by amending its minimum wage regulations, aimed at improving the livelihoods of its workers.

    The minimum salary is not the same in every country. It is typically set based on the cost of living in that country. In Nepal, the current minimum monthly salary was Rs. 15,000 now it has increased to Rs. 17,300.
